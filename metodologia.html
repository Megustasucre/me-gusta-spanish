Our Method

The "Me Gusta" Method

Learning a language, like learning a musical instrument or a new sport, requires constant practice and commitment. After 17 years of teaching Spanish, we know one thing for certain:

Inside the Classroom

How Every Class Works

Each class is pre-planned and tailored to the needs and prior abilities of each student. Our total immersion method has two parts.

1

Theoretical

We explain and practice grammar topics in a logical order, according to the level of difficulty and importance. You understand the structure before you use it.

2

Practical

Reading, songs, games, real-life situations, and cultural activities let you use what you learned. Cognitive processes lead to real language appropriation — not just memorization.

The Science Behind It

How Do We Learn a New Language?

There are countless theories and "formulas" that promise fluency fast. The truth is simpler: learning Spanish should be a conscious, disciplined — and enjoyable — activity.

Children don't study grammar rules — they speak, imitate, and connect sounds to meaning. Our method works the same way: interaction first, rules second.

The Baby Brain Approach

Babies learn by interaction — not by studying rules. We use the same instinct: relate sounds to real things, speak first, analyze later.

Mental Associations

Link new words to images, stories, or sounds you already know. The word escoba (broom)? Think Pablo Escobar. It sticks.

Flashcards

Word on one side, an example or drawing on the other — never just the translation. Context makes vocabulary stick for good.
