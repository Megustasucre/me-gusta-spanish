About Us

Founded with passion in 2005, we've been helping the world fall in love with Spanish and Bolivia ever since.

How It Started

A School Built on One Idea

Me Gusta Spanish was born in 2005, when Elizabeth and Fernando began developing a unique methodology for teaching Spanish — one that would put the student's needs first and weave Bolivian culture into every lesson.

After 6 years of developing and refining their approach, they opened the doors to Me Gusta Spanish School in 2011 in the heart of Sucre — Bolivia's constitutional capital and one of the most beautiful colonial cities in South America.

Today, with 750+ students from over 50 countries and 3 dedicated native teachers, Me Gusta Spanish is recognized as one of the most effective and welcoming Spanish schools in Bolivia.

Who We Are

Mission, Vision & Values

Our Mission

To connect global travelers with the Latin American language and culture through a learning experience that is authentic, welcoming, and professional — positioning Sucre as the ultimate starting point for discovering Spanish and the heart of Latin America.

Our Vision

To become Bolivia's most prestigious institution for Spanish as a Second Language, recognized worldwide for the excellence of our teachers, our deep cultural immersion, and the tangible progress of our students.

Our Values

  • Community — Learning, living, and growing together in a shared space.
  • Quality — Excellence and professionalism in every lesson.
  • Connection — A personal, human, and genuine touch in everything we do.
  • Creativity — Constantly innovating our methods, content, and experiences.
  • Purpose — We believe in a greater «why» behind everything we create.

Our Location

Why Sucre?

Sucre is Bolivia's constitutional capital and a UNESCO World Heritage city — one of the best-preserved colonial cities in Latin America. Its mild climate, rich history, and vibrant culture make it one of the most popular destinations for travelers wanting to learn Spanish.

The Bolivian accent is considered one of the clearest and most neutral in South America, making it ideal for learning Spanish that you can use anywhere in the world.
